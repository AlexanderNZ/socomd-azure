How will the League work?

Tournament Styles will vary based on the game in question, the tournament will be a robust test of a teams ability in their chosen game, we also want it to be a little bit flexible to account for people not being able to make it to every single match - just in case.

League of Legends, Dota 2 and CS:GO matches will be played in a round-robin style, with weekly games leading up to a LAN final in July. FIFA 14 will be a double elimination tournament played on the night.

For more detailed rules, check the sliders to the right. Note that the rules for each league will be subject to change up until one week before start of play.

CS:GO

  • Matches will consist of a single game of 5v5 on de_dust2, within the round robin format.
  • Games will be played on a Tuesday evening at 7pm or 9pm. When you sign up you will be able to indicate your preference for time, we can't guarantee it, but will do our best to find a time everyone is happy with!
  • Each team can have up to two substitute players and will receive one bye week during the season.

Dota 2

  • Matches will be Standard Captains Mode with streaming set to a 3 - 5 minute delay, within the round robin format.
  • Matches will be played on a Wednesday at 8pm.
  • Each team can have up to two substitute players and will receive one bye week during the season.
  • Dota 2 players will be allowed to participate in the LoL tournament, provided they are a registered substitute for that team.
  • We have the ability to be a bit flexible with these matches, teams are welcome to schedule upcoming matches at different times to suit, provided both teams agree to the change and they let us know in advance!

League of Legends

  • Matches will be Blind Mode within the round robin format.
  • Starting sides will be decided by an Overtime Admin, using a coin toss or RNG.
  • Matches will be played on a Thursday at 8pm.
  • Each team can have up to two substitute players and will receive one bye week during the season.
  • LoL players will be allowed to participate in the Dota 2 tournament, provided they are a registered substitute for that team.
  • We have the ability to be a bit flexible with these matches, teams are welcome to schedule upcoming matches at different times to suit, provided both teams agree to the change and they let us know in advance!

FIFA 14

FIFA 14 will be played during the final, as a double elimination event. Rules are yet to be finalised but we'll keep you posted.

Finals in Overtime

Location and Date

The finals will be held at the Spark City Conference center on Friday the third of July, from 6pm until midnight.

Spark City is at 167 Victoria Street West, in the Auckland CBD. The Conference Center is on the Ground Floor, near the stair. It's pretty hard to miss.

If your company isn't Auckland CBD based, you're more then welcome to take part in the league, but if you make it to the finals, it will be up to you to organise travel and stuff like that.

The Dota 2 Finals

The Dota 2 Final will be run after the conclusion of CS:GO and LoL.

The Captains will need to check in no later than 6:30pm - We won't know when the Dota 2 Finals will start until CS:GO and LoL are complete, to be on the safe side make sure you get in early! If you're a member of both the Dota 2 and LoL teams, you will need to be ready for the start of the LoL Final or risk missing out!

Don't forget you have subs! We can try and accomodate everyone, but at the end of the day, it's up to team captains to manage player clashes.

Refreshments and Food

The venue is fully licensed so we'll be running a cash bar for beer and wine. We'll also be selling snacks throughout the night.

Hopefully we have a few surprises for everyone who attends the event!

We all like consuming, but make sure you consume responsibly - don't be that guy from company X who ends up talking to the dinosaurs at 5:15pm.

The League of Legends Finals

League of Legends will be run at the same time as the CS:GO Tournament.

The Captains will need to check in no later than 5:30pm - games will start at 6pm sharp. If you've got players who are on both a LoL and a Dota 2 team, it will be up to you to organise your roster to avoid clashes, there are two subs for each team - make use of them!

Spectator Entry and Ticketing

Entry to the event for competitors will be included in the upfront registration cost. For spectators, there will be a $10 door charge. Competitors who don't make the finals will get free entry to the event!

The CS:GO Finals

The Captains will need to check in no later than 5:30pm - The more timely we are, the more wiggle room we have in case of problems.

The finals map will be de_mirage - Starting sides will be determined by a coin toss.

The Finals Schedule

We'll provide computers and monitors, but bring your peripherals!

The CS:GO Finals will be held once Dota 2 and LoL are all wrapped up. We'll start the CS:GO Finals as soon as we can after those other contests are resolved.

The FIFA 14 Competition will start at 6pm on the night, and be resolved by double elimination until only one competitor remains.

The Fifa 14 Tournament
